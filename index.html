What does "weakened immune system" (immunosuppressed) mean?

Your immune system is your body's defence against infection. Being immunosuppressed means that defence is weaker than normal, so infections can hit you harder. It usually happens for one of two reasons:

  • A health condition — for example HIV, or some blood cancers like leukaemia or lymphoma
  • A treatment — for example chemotherapy, radiotherapy, long-term high-dose steroids, biologic medicines for conditions like rheumatoid arthritis or Crohn's, or the anti-rejection drugs taken after an organ transplant

If you're on regular medication and aren't sure whether it affects your immune system, that's a genuinely common question — pick "Not sure" and ask your GP or pharmacist.
